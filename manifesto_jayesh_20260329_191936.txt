MY OPEN SOURCE PHILOSOPHY
========================================

I, jayesh, believe in the power of open source software.

Every day, I use python — a tool that exists because someone,
somewhere, chose to share their work freely with the world.
This is not merely convenient; it represents a fundamental
belief that knowledge and tools should be accessible to all.

To me, freedom means independance. In the context of software,
this means:

• The freedom to use software for any purpose
• The freedom to study how it works and learn from it
• The freedom to modify it to suit my needs
• The freedom to share improvements with others

The Apache HTTP Server taught us that open source isn't just
about free code — it's about community governance, transparent
development, and the belief that better software emerges when
we build together. For 25 years, Apache has powered the web,
proving that a community of volunteers can create something
that rivals and surpasses corporate alternatives.

I commit to building mobile phone and sharing it openly. Because
innovation doesn't happen in isolation — it happens when we
stand on the shoulders of giants, improve what came before,
and pass those improvements forward.

"Standing on the shoulders of giants" is not just a metaphor.
In open source, it is the literal reality of every line of
code I will ever write. The giants are the developers who
chose to share under licenses like the Apache License 2.0,
the GPL, and the MIT License — licenses that protect freedom
while enabling collaboration.

Open source ensures those shoulders belong to everyone.

This is my manifesto. I choose to build in the open.
